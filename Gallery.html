HOW WAS I INTRODUCE TO WEB DEVELOPMENT

I was introduced to web development through curiosity and a bit of necessity. It all began when I stumbled across a basic HTML tutorial while trying to create a simple personal blog. Intrigued by how lines
of code could transform into a functioning webpage, I dove deeper. I started with HTML and CSS, experimenting with colors, fonts, and layouts. Soon, I discovered JavaScript and realized how interactivity could bring a site to life. Each new skill felt like unlocking a new superpower, and
I was hooked. What started as a casual interest quickly turned into a passion, shaping the way I view technology and creativity today.

How I Became a Software Developer

My journey to becoming a software developer was a blend of curiosity, persistence, and a passion for problem-solving. It all started with a fascination for technology and the way it shapes our world. I remember tinkering with computers and wondering how programs worked behind the scenes. Driven by this curiosity, I began exploring programming through online tutorials and coding challenges. Starting with the basics of Python, I fell in love with how logic and creativity combined to solve problems. From there, I learned more languages and frameworks, building small projects like to-do apps and simple games. What truly solidified my path was a project that solved a real-world issue for my community. Seeing people use something I created inspired me to pursue software development as a career. Formal studies and practical experience further honed my skills, and I embraced opportunities to collaborate on larger projects. Becoming a software developer wasn’t just about learning to code—it was about perseverance, embracing challenges, and a constant desire to learn and improve. Today, it feels incredible to be part of an ever-evolving field that shapes the future.
